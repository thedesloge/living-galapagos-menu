Evolving Paradise
In Galapagos, Man and Nature Compete for Survival

The Galápagos Islands are one of the earth’s most unique and beautiful natural environments. Their volcanic origin and geographic isolation combine to create an unparalleled living showcase of biodiversity. Yet they are not the unspoiled paradise that many imagine. The number of tourists rose from 40,000 in 1990 to 145,000 in 1996, a gain of more than 250%. There are now an estimated 30,000 permanent residents. Sustaining a growing human population while preserving the natural environment has proven to be a unique and evolving challenge.

Living Galápagos is a student-authored multimedia website that explores the impact of mankind on the Galápagos Islands. Produced by the University of North Carolina School of Journalism and Mass Communication, this ongoing project offers a mix of human interest and science-related content, offering education, awareness and entertainment for audiences worldwide. Originally published in 2009, Living Galápagos is bilingual and features a searchable database of content dating back to the launch year.
